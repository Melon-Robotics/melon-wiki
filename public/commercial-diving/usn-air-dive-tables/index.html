WARNING: Altitudes above 10,000 feet can impose serious stress on the body resulting in significant medical problems while the acclimatization process takes place. Ascents to these altitudes must be slow to allow acclimatization to occur and prophylactic drugs may be required to prevent the occurrence of altitude sickness. These exposures should always be planned in consultation with a Diving Medical Officer. Commands conducting diving operations above 10,000 feet may obtain the appropriate decompression procedures from NAVSEA 00C.

Notes:

  1. When using Table 9-6, use the highest repetitive group designator obtained in the previous 24-hour period.
  2. Table 9-6 may only be used when the maximum altitude achieved is 10,000 feet or less. For ascents above 10,000 feet, consult NAVSEA 00C for guidance.
  3. The cabin pressure in commercial aircraft is maintained at a constant value regardless of the actual altitude of the flight. Though cabin pressure varies somewhat with aircraft type, the nominal value is 8,000 feet. For commercial flights, use a final altitude of 8,000 feet to compute the required surface interval before flying.
  4. No surface interval is required before taking a commercial flight if the dive site is at 8,000 feet or higher. In this case, flying results in an increase in atmospheric pressure rather than a decrease.
  5. For ascent to altitude following a non-saturation helium-oxygen dive, wait 12 hours if the dive was a no-decompression dive. Wait 24 hours if the dive was a decompression dive.

Frequently Asked Questions#

What is the no-decompression limit at 60 feet for air diving?#

Per USN Table 9-7, the NDL at 60 FSW on air is 63 minutes (Group A diver). With residual nitrogen from previous dives, use Table 9-8 to calculate adjusted bottom time.

What is the no-decompression limit at 100 feet?#

Per USN Table 9-7, the NDL at 100 FSW on air is 25 minutes (Group A diver). Decompression stops are required beyond this time.

How do I calculate a repetitive dive?#

  1. Find your post-dive repetitive group from Table 9-7 (depth + bottom time)
  2. Use Table 9-8 Part 1 (previous group + surface interval) → new group
  3. Use Table 9-8 Part 2 (new group + next dive depth) → Residual Nitrogen Time (RNT)
  4. Add RNT to planned bottom time → Equivalent Single Dive Time (ESDT)
  5. If ESDT > NDL, use Table 9-9 for decompression schedule

Use the interactive tool above to perform steps 1–4 automatically.

What is the source of these tables?#

SS521-AG-PRO-010, U.S. Navy Diving Manual, Revision 7, 01 December 2016 Published by Direction of Commander, Naval Sea Systems Command (NAVSEA)
